Walter Pritchard — Senior VP, Investor Relations and Corporate Development

Good day, everyone, and welcome to Palo Alto Networks' second quarter 2025 earnings conference
call. With me on today's call: Nikesh Arora, Chairman and CEO; Dipak Golechha, CFO. Following
prepared remarks, Lee Klarich, Chief Product Officer, will join for Q&A.

All per-share figures adjusted for the 2-for-1 stock split effected after close of trading on
December 12, 2024.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Nikesh Arora — Chairman and CEO
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

We made considerable progress in platformization, allowing us to outperform both our top and
bottom-line expectations for this quarter. We delivered on our high ARPU expectations toward the
top of the range. This gave us strength in our NGS ARR and also allowed us to outperform our
revenue expectations. Growth was pretty broad across the entire portfolio with strength across
all three geographies and platforms.

Strong performance from large deals internationally and strong contribution from SASE, software
firewalls, and XSIAM. On the profitability front, we delivered operating margins ahead of our
internal target despite some one-time events. Efficiency initiatives continue to bear fruit,
including some promising early contributions from AI.

From our vantage point, the outlook for cybersecurity seems to have been robust in Q2 and is
likely to stay so over the rest of this year.

As the conversation around AI continues to get omnipresent and companies race to evaluate,
experiment, and deploy AI, they're discovering that some of the legacy architectures come in
the way of their aspirations. This is resulting in a resurgence of cloud transformation projects
and consequently, demand for network security transformation. While cybersecurity is a derivative
effect, it is clear that the longer-term trend toward AI is going to continue to underpin
technology transformation.

We see heightened interest in data security posture management, where our acquisition of Dig
seems to be proving prescient. Cloud infrastructure is much more dynamic than on-prem IT
creating risk. As cloud data volumes grow, customers are doubling down on protecting their cloud
environments.

- Total of over 1,150 platformizations within our top 5,000 customers
- Two-platform customers grew over 50% in Q2

- Number of customers platformized in Cortex up more than 3x
- On track to achieve stated target of 2,500 to 3,500 platformizations by FY2030

Dipak Golechha — CFO
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Q2 FINANCIALS:
- Total revenue: $2.26 billion, grew 14% (above high end of guidance)
- Product revenue: grew 8% (approaching 40% software on TTM basis)
- Services revenue: grew 16% (subscription +20%, support +8%)
- Gross margin: 76.6% — down slightly; driven by (a) newer SaaS offerings not yet at scale,
  (b) one-time inventory/product transition costs (~40 bps) that won't recur in H2
- Non-GAAP operating margin: 28.4% — delivered ahead of internal target
- OpEx as % of revenue: decreased 120 bps (scale + efficiency culture)
- Non-GAAP EPS: $0.81 diluted (GAAP EPS $0.38)
- Adjusted FCF: >$509M in Q2

GEOGRAPHIC:
- Americas: +13%, EMEA: +18%, JPAC: +17%
- Largest-ever deals in both EMEA and JPAC, each >$50M

RPO / NGS ARR:
- Total RPO: $13.0B, grew 21% (high end of guidance)
- Current RPO: $6.1B, grew 17%
- Average contract duration: ~3 years (trended toward high end due to large XSIAM deals)
- NGS ARR: $4.78B, grew 37% (driven by advanced subs, SASE, Cortex)

FCF AND DEFERRED PAYMENTS:
- FY2024 entered year with $1.0B deferred payments (32% of FY24 FCF)
- FY2025: entered year with $1.4B deferred payments (41% of expected FY25 FCF)
- FY2026 guidance: expect to exit with $2.0B deferred payments — "further increasing visibility"
- Confident in FCF margins >37% for FY2026 and FY2027
- FY2025 FCF will be more back-end loaded (more in Q4) vs prior years

AI EFFICIENCY INITIATIVES:
- Contract labor reduction: on track for ~50% reduction by end of FY2025 (direct OpEx savings)
- Customer support copilot: used in ~85% of NetSec support cases; ~50% reduction in resolution time
- Developer copilots: deployed to all engineers

PLATFORMIZATION PROGRAM UPDATE (1-year anniversary):
- Embedded practices into standard sales motion
- Ecosystem partners (GSIs) putting significant resources behind platformization
- ~1/3 of all sales reps have participated in a new platformization deal win in last 12 months

QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS
================================================================================

SAKET KALIA (Barclays) — FCF sustainability and deferred payment dynamics
Nikesh: "Platform deals are actually a lot more efficient from a sales perspective because the
larger deals — 50-60% of P&L is sales, marketing, and customer support. If you can find
efficiencies in that process, that's where leverage lies."
Dipak: Confirmed approaching end of deferred payment transition — "there's not as much left to
actually do. We're quite far along the journey."

HAMZA FODDERWALA (Morgan Stanley) — DeepSeek impact
Nikesh: Viewed as "phenomenal pivotal moment." DeepSeek at 2% of cost of other models drives
experimentation. Security imperative: deploy with firewalls, sequestered environment, AI
guardrails. "Any new technology requires you to put more security around it."

BRIAN ESSEX (JPMorgan) — AI for security, win rates vs. point solutions
Nikesh: "Is any point solution business left? Every security company seems to claim they're
platform now." Real advantage: platformized customers have harmonized data; can build agents
on top of 1,150 customer data sets.
Lee Klarich: Cortex Cloud connects AppSec through cloud through runtime through SOC — "that
is incredibly powerful."
On win rates: Yes, confirmed improvement vs. point solution competitors.
Nikesh: "The bigger cloud security action is going to be in runtime with agents... it's actually
not the CNAPP players who have had — who won the last round."

GABRIELA BORGES (Goldman Sachs) — Guardrails on deferred payment deals
Dipak: Higher-end deals drive deferred payment requests. Hardware always upfront. Sales comp
and approval guardrails in place. "Enabling platformization at scale."

JONATHAN HO (William Blair) — Rationale for CNAPP merging into Cortex
Lee Klarich: Runtime security requires CNAPP context. Cleaner cloud posture = easier for AI to
detect real attacks. "Best outcome for customers achieved when they connect all aspects of cloud
together."

PETER WEED (Bernstein) — Product revenue durability
Nikesh: Hardware expected to deliver "low to mid-single digits" sustainably. Real growth driver
is software firewalls and SASE replacing hardware mix. Cloud volume going up faster than data
center volumes declining. Strategy is managing transition to higher-margin software.

SHAUL EYAL (Cowen) — IBM/QRadar partnership
Nikesh: "One day, a Harvard Business School case would be written on this for the right reasons.
This has been a spectacular partner for us." Asian bank deal example: was QRadar customer, ARR
went up 5x. "It couldn't have been better."

TAL LIANI (BofA) — Gross margin and operating margin
Dipak: Gross margin pressure from (1) faster growth of newer SaaS (not yet at scale) and
(2) one-time inventory write-offs (~40 bps) on hardware — "will not repeat in H2."
Nikesh: "We still outperformed our margin expectations, both internally and as for you, guys."

ANDREW NOWINSKI (Wells Fargo) — Net new ARR declining ex-QRadar [BEAR CASE]
KEY EXCHANGE: "If you pull out the $74M from QRadar in Q1, it looks like your net new ARR
declined on a year-over-year basis for the last two consecutive quarters."
Dipak: Acknowledged but attributed to lapping a large step-up in cloud-delivered advanced
subscription attaches from a year ago. "The net new ARR on some of our newer products, what's
driving a lot of the platformization continues to go from strength to strength."
Nikesh: "Six years ago, this was zero. We're very happy that it's driving up closer to $5B.
We still believe we are on track to get to $15B on NGS ARR."

MATTHEW HEDBERG (RBC) — Agentic AI security
Lee Klarich: Identity is "not a magic bullet" — also need to secure the AI environment itself,
machine-to-machine communications, and agentic actions with AI-enabled SOC.
Nikesh: XSIAM sold >200 times in 24 months — fastest-growing product in cybersecurity. Expects
to build agentic detection, remediation, and management within XSIAM.
