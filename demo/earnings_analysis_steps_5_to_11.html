Earnings analysis Steps 5 through 11: detailed sub-dimensions for each analytical step

Phase 2 — Analysis: Steps 5–8
5 Beat / Miss Analysis
Metrics scored
  • Revenue
  • Gross margin (bps)
  • EBITDA / Op. profit
  • EPS — adj. + GAAP
  • Key biz metrics (ARR, billings, etc.)
For every beat or miss, ask
  • What specifically drove it?
  • One-time or recurring?
  • Did mgmt guide accordingly?
  • Thesis impact?
Output Variance table: Reported vs. Our Est vs. Consensus + narrative for each line
6 Segment / Geo / Product
Dimensions to cut
  • Business segment
  • Geography (NA, EMEA, APAC, China)
  • Product category / SKU
  • Channel (direct, wholesale, e-comm)
For each dimension
  • What outperformed vs. est?
  • What underperformed?
  • QoQ and YoY trend
  • Mgmt forward commentary
Output Segment waterfall — where the beat or miss actually came from
7 Margin Analysis
Margin lines examined
  • Gross margin
  • Operating margin
  • Net margin
Driver decomposition
  • Pricing (ASP change?)
  • Mix (higher/lower margin products?)
  • Cost structure (COGS, R&D, S&M)
  • Operating leverage (scale?)
Forward question
  • Is expansion structural or temporary?
  • Management's margin commitment?
Output Margin bridge: prior Q → current Q, with driver attribution
8 Guidance Analysis
If guidance provided
  • Raised / lowered / maintained?
  • Delta vs. prior guidance
  • Delta vs. Street consensus
  • Key assumptions disclosed?
Credibility check
  • Track record: sandbagger or stretch?
  • Implied growth decel or reaccel?
  • Any hedging language in tone?
If no guidance given
  • Flag explicitly
  • Derive independent outlook from results + commentary
Output Guidance delta table + credibility read + implied Street revision direction

Phase 2 continued — Model, Valuation & Rating: Steps 9–11
9 Update Financial Model
What gets updated
  • Drop actuals into current quarter
  • Revise remaining quarters (current FY)
  • Revise next FY estimates
  • Year +2 if relevant
Estimate change table format
  • Old Est → New Est → Δ% → Reason
  • Revenue, EBITDA, EPS each row
  • Current FY + next FY
  • Every cell traceable to a source
Output Old vs. new estimate table — the single most-read section of any earnings note
10 Valuation & Price Target
Valuation refresh
  • Recalc DCF with new cash flow est.
  • Update comps multiples (if peers reported)
  • Derive new fair value range
Price target decision rules
  • >5% estimate change → usually move PT
  • <5% change → may hold PT
  • Thesis shift → may move PT even if small Δ
Output New price target (or explicit hold) + implied upside / downside from current price
11 Assess Rating Impact
Decision triggers
  • Strong beat + guidance raised → consider upgrade
  • Miss + guidance cut → consider downgrade
  • Mixed / inline → usually maintain
Three factors to weigh
  • Stock reaction (market already moved?)
  • Valuation vs. new estimates (cheap/rich?)
  • Risk/reward asymmetry (skew shifted?)
Output Rating: Maintained / Upgraded / Downgraded — with explicit rationale tied to the above
