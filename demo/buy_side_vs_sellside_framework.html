1 Investment Horizon What timeframe does Buy / Hold / Sell refer to?
Not addressed
  • Implicitly assumes 12-month PT
  • No horizon stated or varied
  • Rating is convention, not explicit
  • State horizon upfront — it governs everything that follows
  • Short (<3 mo): guidance vs. consensus, gap fill
  • Medium (6–18 mo): margin expansion, RPO trajectory
  • Long (2–5 yr): moat durability, TAM capture
  • Different horizons → different signals matter

2 Alpha Edge What does the market have wrong?
Partial
  • Beat/miss vs. consensus (Steps 5–8)
  • Thesis impact assessed (Step 11)
  • But consensus = known, not alpha
  • What is the market mispricing? Not just beat/miss
  • Which metric is being over/under-weighted in the reaction?
  • What did mgmt signal that analysts glossed over?
  • For PANW: is platformization compounding faster than the multiple implies?
In transcript Analyst prior

AI can do: Surface what mgmt emphasized vs. downplayed; flag analyst Q&A themes

3 Peer Context How does this quarter look relative to the competitive set?
Partial

  • No customer-behavior lens
  • Relative performance vs. CRWD, FTNT, ZS

4 Sentiment & Positioning Who is set up how? (Not information — positioning)
Not in skill
  • Entirely absent from framework
  • No market reaction analysis
  • No positioning or flow signals
  • Trader sentiment: options skew, put/call ratio, short interest Δ
  • Insider signal: Form 4 filings — buying or selling into the print?
  • Positioning: who is over/underweight creates asymmetric reactions
  • Key distinction: this is about positioning, not information edge
External data

AI can do: Summarize Form 4 filings, parse options commentary if fed the data

AI can't do: Access live market microstructure without a market data connector
Key demo insight: Steps 1–4 (data collection) and Steps 9–11 (model/rating) are largely mechanical — AI handles them well with the right inputs. The live action is in the analytical core (Steps 5–8) combined with your three additions. The AI brings speed and structure; you bring the horizon declaration, the alpha thesis, the peer lens, and the positioning read. That division of labor is exactly what the workshop should make visceral.
