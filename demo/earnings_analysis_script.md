## IMPORTANT: What This Script Is

This script uses **Claude directly in Cowork**. It does NOT use the Earnings Reviewer plugin from the financial-services-plugins repository. That plugin is not installed on the demo machine.

The analytical framework in Steps 5–8 below mirrors the Earnings Reviewer skill's structure — beat/miss, segment breakdown, margin analysis, guidance read — but is executed by pasting structured data into a Cowork session and prompting Claude directly. Any departure from the standard skill workflow is called out explicitly with a **[DEPARTURE]** tag.

All outputs are:
- Generated by Claude from structured data extracted from the earnings database (`demo/data/db/earnings.db`)
- Not pre-written, not fabricated, not attributed to any plugin we did not run
- Produced live in the Cowork session during the demo

### STEP 5: Beat/Miss Analysis

**What the Earnings Reviewer skill does here:** Compares actual results to consensus estimates across revenue, EPS, and key operating metrics. Flags surprises. Reads market reaction vs. fundamental performance.

**[DEPARTURE]:** The standard skill ingests a transcript and filings simultaneously. We are pasting structured data directly. The analytical content is equivalent — the input format is different.

**Paste this prompt:**

```
You are a buy-side analyst reviewing PANW's Q3 FY26 earnings.

Using only the data I've provided above:

1. Summarize the beat/miss picture: revenue ($3,002M vs $2,940M consensus), EPS Non-GAAP
   ($0.85 vs $0.800 consensus), and the platform KPIs. Be precise — state the actual number,
   the consensus, and the magnitude in dollars and percent.

2. The stock fell -4.44% after hours despite beating on revenue, EPS, and raising FY26 guidance.
   What in the data explains that reaction? Look carefully at the gap between reported NGS ARR
   growth (+60%) and organic growth (+28% ex-CyberArk/Chronosphere).

3. What is the single most important number in this print — the one a buy-side analyst would
   be anchoring their view on? Explain why.

Keep this tight: three short paragraphs, no headers needed.
```

### STEP 6: Segment and Platform Breakdown

**What the Earnings Reviewer skill does here:** Breaks down revenue by business line or geography, identifies which segments are accelerating vs. decelerating, and flags mix shift trends.

**[DEPARTURE]:** PANW does not report traditional segment revenue in the same way as a product company. The relevant "segments" are product revenue vs. subscription/support, plus the platform KPIs (NGS ARR, Platformized Customers, RPO).

**[DEPARTURE — GEOGRAPHY]:** Geographic revenue split is not in our data. The standard skill would include it. Skip or note its absence — the 10-Q (when filed) will have it.

**Paste this prompt:**

```
Still working with the PANW Q3 FY26 data above.

PANW's business is best understood through its platformization thesis — the shift from selling
point products to consolidating customer security spending onto one platform.

1. Walk through the key platform metrics: NGS ARR $8.13B (+60% reported, +28% organic),
   Platformized Customers 2,280, RPO $18.4B (+36% reported, ~+22% organic). What does each
   tell us about the health of the platformization thesis? Is the data confirming, stalling,
   or contradicting the thesis?

2. The revenue mix is $594M product vs. $2,408M subscription/support (80.2% subscription).
   Both grew ~31% YoY. What does it tell us that product and subscription are growing at
   the same rate this quarter? Is the platformization mix-shift narrative being borne out?

3. The gap between reported NGS ARR (+60%) and organic NGS ARR (+28%) is ~32 percentage
   points — that's the CyberArk + Chronosphere acquisition contribution. How should a
   long-term investor weight reported vs. organic for the platformization thesis?

Be analytical and direct. Point to specific numbers. Flag what's genuinely uncertain.
```

## THE DECISION LAYER (MOVE 2 — Run live in Cowork after Steps 5–8)

This section is structural only. The actual analysis happens live in Cowork during the demo. No pre-written conclusions below.

### Dimension 1: Investment Horizon Declaration

**What it does:** Investment horizon changes every signal that matters. A 90-day trader reads the -4.44% AH reaction and the Q4 step-up very differently from an 18-month position holder who cares about platformization compounding.

**Prompt card (Move 1, after Steps 5–8):**

```
Before we interpret what we just found, let's declare a horizon.

Scenario A — 90-day trade: What is the most likely price path from $297 (Jun 2 close) /
~$284 (Jun 3 open) into the next print? What are the two or three things that would
drive that path — beat magnitude, organic ARR trajectory, CyberArk integration milestones?

Scenario B — 18-month position: The question is whether platformization is compounding
fast enough to justify the 18.8x NTM multiple. Based on Q3 data alone (+28% organic NGS
ARR, raised FY26 guide, FCF margin guide 37.5%), what's your conviction level (1–10) and
what's the single biggest uncertainty?

Run both. Show me where they diverge. That's the interesting question.
```

### Dimension 2: Alpha Edge

**What it does:** Not beat/miss vs. consensus. What is the market mispricing?

**Prompt card:**

```
Set aside the beat/miss. The stock fell -4.44% on a quarter that beat on both revenue
and EPS, with raised FY26 guidance and a Q4 step-up.

What is the market worried about that isn't in the headline numbers? Based on the call
exchanges — particularly the 6-of-9 "partial" responses — what's the bear case the
street is pricing? Is it the gap between reported NGS ARR (60%) and organic (28%)?
Is it the GAAP OI -$183M raising integration cost concerns? Is it positioning unwind
on a crowded long?

Then: PANW is at 18.8x NTM EV/Rev growing organic 28%. CRWD is at 34.2x TTM EV/Rev
growing 23%. ZS is at ~18x growing 25%. FTNT is at ~12x growing 20%. Where does
PANW sit on the growth-vs-multiple curve, and what does that imply for relative
positioning?
```

### Dimension 3: Peer Context

**What it does:** Relative performance vs. CRWD, FTNT, ZS. Who is winning the platform consolidation trade?

**Prompt card:**

## APPENDIX A: DB QUERIES FOR DATA EXTRACTION

Run these against `demo/data/db/earnings.db` to regenerate the data package for any quarter.

```python
import sqlite3
conn = sqlite3.connect('demo/data/db/earnings.db')

TARGET_PERIOD = 'Q3_FY26'  # Change this for rerun
TARGET_SYMBOL = 'PANW'

# Quarterly financials
conn.execute(f"SELECT * FROM quarterly_financials WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}' AND fiscal_period='{TARGET_PERIOD}'").fetchall()

# KPIs
conn.execute(f"SELECT kpi_name, kpi_value, kpi_unit, kpi_label FROM company_kpis WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}' AND fiscal_period='{TARGET_PERIOD}' ORDER BY kpi_name").fetchall()

# Consensus estimates
conn.execute(f"SELECT * FROM consensus_estimates WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}' AND fiscal_period='{TARGET_PERIOD}'").fetchall()

# Guidance
conn.execute(f"SELECT issued_for_period, metric, low_value, high_value, unit, revision_vs_prior, analytical_note FROM guidance WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}' ORDER BY id").fetchall()

# EPS history (last 8)
conn.execute(f"SELECT fiscal_date_ending, eps_nongaap_actual, eps_nongaap_estimate, eps_surprise_pct FROM eps_history WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}' ORDER BY fiscal_date_ending DESC LIMIT 8").fetchall()

# Transcript Q&A
conn.execute(f"SELECT exchange_num, analyst_name, analyst_firm, topics, key_signal, analytical_note FROM transcript_qa WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}' AND fiscal_period='{TARGET_PERIOD}' ORDER BY exchange_num").fetchall()

# Sentiment signals
conn.execute(f"SELECT signal_type, value, unit, context_note FROM sentiment_signals WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}'").fetchall()

# Insider transactions (window: 4 months pre-print → print date)
conn.execute(f"SELECT filing_date, insider_name, insider_role, transaction_code, shares, price_per_share, total_value_m, notes FROM insider_transactions WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}' AND filing_date BETWEEN '2026-02-01' AND '2026-06-02' ORDER BY filing_date").fetchall()

# Peer financials (most recent quarter per peer)
conn.execute("SELECT symbol, fiscal_period, revenue_total_m, revenue_yoy_growth_pct, operating_margin_nongaap_pct, fcf_m FROM quarterly_financials WHERE company_type='peer'").fetchall()

# Peer KPIs
conn.execute("SELECT symbol, kpi_name, kpi_value, kpi_unit FROM company_kpis WHERE company_type='peer' ORDER BY symbol, kpi_name").fetchall()
```
