The analytical framework in Steps 5–8 below mirrors the Earnings Reviewer skill's structure — beat/miss, segment breakdown, margin analysis, guidance read — but is executed by pasting structured data into a Cowork session and prompting Claude directly. Any departure from the standard skill workflow is called out explicitly with a **[DEPARTURE]** tag.

All outputs are:
- Generated by Claude from structured data extracted from the earnings database (`/tmp/earnings_v2.db`)
- Not pre-written, not fabricated, not attributed to any plugin we did not run

### STEP 5: Beat/Miss Analysis

**What the Earnings Reviewer skill does here:** Compares actual results to consensus estimates across revenue, EPS, and key operating metrics. Flags surprises. Reads market reaction vs. fundamental performance.

**[DEPARTURE]:** The standard skill ingests a transcript and filings simultaneously. We are pasting structured data directly. The analytical content is equivalent — the input format is different.

**Paste this prompt:**

```
You are a buy-side analyst reviewing PANW's Q2 FY26 earnings.

Using only the data I've provided above:

1. Summarize the beat/miss picture: revenue, EPS (GAAP and non-GAAP), and any key operating metrics vs. expectations. Be precise — state the actual number, the consensus, and the direction.

2. The stock fell -3.5% after hours despite the beats. What in the data explains that reaction? Look at the guidance carefully.

3. What is the single most important number in this print — the one a buy-side analyst would be anchoring their view on? Explain why.

Keep this tight: three short paragraphs, no headers needed.
```

---

### STEP 6: Segment and Platform Breakdown

**What the Earnings Reviewer skill does here:** Breaks down revenue by business line or geography, identifies which segments are accelerating vs. decelerating, and flags mix shift trends.

**[DEPARTURE]:** PANW does not report traditional segment revenue in the same way as a product company. The relevant "segments" for PANW are product revenue vs. subscription/support, plus the platform KPIs (NGS ARR, Platformized Customers, RPO). Geographic breakdown is NOT available from the press release data we have — the 10-Q would have it.

**[DEPARTURE — GEOGRAPHY]:** Geographic revenue split is not in our data. The standard skill would include it. Skip or note its absence.

**Paste this prompt:**

```
Still working with the PANW Q2 FY26 data above.

PANW's business is best understood through its platformization thesis — the shift from selling point products to consolidating customer security spending onto one platform.

1. Walk through the key platform metrics: NGS ARR, Platformized Customers, RPO. What does each tell us about the health of the platformization thesis? Is the data confirming, stalling, or contradicting the thesis?

2. The revenue mix is $421.5M product vs. $1,835.9M subscription/support. What is this mix shift telling us about the business model transition? Is this what you'd want to see?

3. Exchange 9 from the call (Wells Fargo/Nowinski) raises a specific concern about organic net new ARR ex-QRadar declining for two consecutive quarters. What would you need to see in Q3 to either confirm this as a structural issue or dismiss it as noise?

Be analytical and direct. Point to specific numbers. Flag what's genuinely uncertain.
```

### STEP 7: Margin Analysis

**What the Earnings Reviewer skill does here:** Traces the gross-to-operating-to-FCF margin stack. Identifies what's driving margin expansion or compression. Assesses durability.

**Paste this prompt:**

```
Now focus on the margin story for PANW Q2 FY26.

1. Map the margin stack: gross margin (GAAP 73.5% / Non-GAAP 76.6%) → operating margin (GAAP 10.6% / Non-GAAP 28.4%) → FCF margin (22.5%). What is the gap between Non-GAAP and GAAP operating income telling us? The SBC is $343.3M — put that in context.

2. GAAP operating income grew +348.5% YoY. Is this a real inflection or a base effect? How do you evaluate this number?

3. Q3 guidance calls for non-GAAP EPS of $0.76–$0.77, below Q2's actual $0.81. Exchange 8 (BofA) flagged two gross margin headwinds — structural SaaS scale and a one-time inventory item. How do you read the sequential EPS step-down in guidance? Is this a margin trap or a timing effect?

4. FCF margin is 22.5% this quarter vs. FY26 guidance of 37–38%. What explains the gap? (Hint: look at the deferred revenue and what Kalia/Barclays flagged in exchange 1.)

One paragraph per question. Specific numbers throughout.
```

### STEP 8: Guidance Read

**What the Earnings Reviewer skill does here:** Reads management's forward guidance vs. prior guidance, vs. street estimates, and assesses credibility. Flags sandbagging vs. genuine deceleration signals.

**Paste this prompt:**

```
Read the guidance section of the PANW Q2 FY26 data carefully.

1. FY26 full-year EPS guidance was raised ($3.18–$3.24). Revenue guidance was maintained ($9.14–$9.19B). But Q3 EPS guidance ($0.76–$0.77) is below Q2's actual $0.81, and Q3 NGS ARR guidance ($5.03–$5.08B) implies 33–34% growth vs. Q2's 37%. What is the overall guidance tone — raise/maintain/cut on each dimension?

2. Is the Q3 EPS step-down ($0.81 → $0.77) a guidance trap or consistent with seasonality? What would you need to know to distinguish between them?

3. PANW has a multi-quarter history of beating estimates (7 of the last 8 quarters on EPS). Does that history change how you read the guidance level set? What's the implied beat scenario vs. the implied risk scenario?

4. If you had to flag one number in the guidance that you'd watch most closely in the Q3 print, what is it and why?

Keep this crisp. This is the setup for an investment view, not a research note.
```

## THE BUY-SIDE LAYER (MOVE 2 — Run live in Cowork after June 3 Q3 data)

This section is structural only. The actual analysis happens live in Cowork during the demo. No pre-written conclusions below.

### Dimension 1: Investment Horizon Declaration

**What it does:** Investment horizon changes every signal that matters. A 90-day trader reads the -3.5% AH reaction and the guidance step-down very differently from an 18-month position holder who cares about platformization compounding.

**Prompt card (Move 1, after Steps 5–8):**

```
Before we interpret what we just found, let's declare a horizon.

Scenario A — 90-day trade: What is the most likely price path from here to the Q3 print? What are the two or three things that would drive that path?

Scenario B — 18-month position: The question is whether platformization is compounding fast enough to justify the multiple. Based on Q2 data alone, what's your conviction level (1–10) and what's the single biggest uncertainty?

Run both. Show me where they diverge. That's the interesting question.
```

### Dimension 2: Alpha Edge

**What it does:** Not beat/miss vs. consensus. What is the market mispricing?

**Prompt card:**

```
Set aside the beat/miss. The stock is down -3.5% on a quarter that beat on both revenue and EPS.

What is the market worried about that isn't in the consensus model? Based on the call exchanges — particularly Nowinski's question in exchange 9 — what is the bear case the street hasn't fully priced?

Then: Is PANW's platformization compounding faster or slower than the multiple implies? The stock is trading at [X]x forward revenue. What does the NGS ARR growth rate and the RPO tell you about whether that multiple is earned?
```

### Dimension 3: Peer Context

**What it does:** Relative performance vs. CRWD, FTNT, ZS. Who is winning the platform consolidation trade?

**Prompt card:**

```
Here are the most recent results for PANW's three closest peers in the cybersecurity platform space:
[paste peer data block from the data package above]

1. On revenue growth: CRWD +23%, FTNT +20%, ZS +25%, PANW +14.3%. PANW has the lowest growth rate. Does that matter? What explains it and is it a signal or noise?

2. On margins: FTNT non-GAAP OI margin is 35.8% vs. PANW's 28.4%. FTNT is more profitable per dollar of revenue. Is that a competitive signal or a business model difference?

3. ZS just missed revenue consensus. Does that tell you anything about the demand environment that affects how you read PANW's guidance?

4. Who is winning the platform consolidation trade right now — based purely on numbers, not narrative?
```

### Dimension 4: Sentiment and Positioning

**What it does:** Explains asymmetric reactions to the same print. Not information edge — positioning.

**Prompt card (Move 2):**

```

[paste sentiment/insider block from the data package above]

1. The stock fell -3.5% after hours and then recovered to $208 within 6 trading days. What does the CEO's block sale timing tell you — or not tell you — about insider conviction?

2. Low short interest (~1.5% of float), elevated put buying pre-print, rapid recovery post-print. Is this dip-and-rip consistent with the fundamental read from Steps 5–8, or is it disconnected from fundamentals?
```

## APPENDIX A: DB QUERIES FOR DATA EXTRACTION

Run these against `/tmp/earnings_v2.db` to regenerate the data package for any quarter.

```python
import sqlite3
conn = sqlite3.connect('/tmp/earnings_v2.db')

TARGET_PERIOD = 'Q3_FY26'  # Change this for rerun
TARGET_SYMBOL = 'PANW'

# Quarterly financials
conn.execute(f"SELECT * FROM quarterly_financials WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}' AND fiscal_period='{TARGET_PERIOD}'").fetchall()

# KPIs
conn.execute(f"SELECT kpi_name, kpi_value, kpi_unit, kpi_label FROM company_kpis WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}' ORDER BY kpi_name").fetchall()

# Consensus estimates
conn.execute(f"SELECT * FROM consensus_estimates WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}' AND fiscal_period='{TARGET_PERIOD}'").fetchall()

# Guidance
conn.execute(f"SELECT issued_for_period, metric, low_value, high_value, unit, revision_vs_prior, analytical_note FROM guidance WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}' ORDER BY id").fetchall()

# EPS history (last 8)
conn.execute(f"SELECT fiscal_date_ending, eps_gaap_actual, eps_gaap_estimated, eps_surprise_pct FROM eps_history WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}' ORDER BY fiscal_date_ending DESC LIMIT 8").fetchall()

# Transcript Q&A
conn.execute(f"SELECT exchange_num, analyst_name, analyst_firm, topics, key_signal, analytical_note FROM transcript_qa WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}' ORDER BY exchange_num").fetchall()

# Sentiment signals
conn.execute(f"SELECT signal_type, value, unit, context_note FROM sentiment_signals WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}'").fetchall()

# Insider transactions
conn.execute(f"SELECT filing_date, insider_name, insider_role, transaction_type, shares, price_per_share, total_value_m, is_10b5_1_plan, notes FROM insider_transactions WHERE symbol='{TARGET_SYMBOL}'").fetchall()

# Peer financials (most recent quarter per peer)
conn.execute("SELECT symbol, fiscal_period, revenue_total_m, revenue_yoy_growth_pct, operating_margin_nongaap_pct, fcf_m FROM quarterly_financials WHERE company_type='peer'").fetchall()

# Peer KPIs
conn.execute("SELECT symbol, kpi_name, kpi_value, kpi_unit FROM company_kpis WHERE company_type='peer' ORDER BY symbol, kpi_name").fetchall()
```
